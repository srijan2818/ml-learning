 # Gaussian Mixture Models + Expectation Maximisation 

GMM assumes that data points are generated by a mixture of simple distributions, where the identity of the 
way the distributions are generated / generating component is hidden

- Data-generating process not geometry
- Density not distance
- Probabilistic not geometry



The likelihood is log of a sum  →  $\log \sum_k \pi_k p(x|\theta_k)$<br>
Since log of a sum cant be distributed , components are coupled and points cant be assigned clearly

EM introduces a hidden variable - the generating component<br>
If we knew this variable optimisation becomes meaningless, parameters decouple<br>
EM alternated between - 
1) Soft assumption that we know that variable
2) Re-optimize the parameters under that pretense by maximizing the lower bound on the true objective - the log likelihood

- Log likelihood never decreases but global optimum is not guaranteed
- Makes covariance collapse possible when we single in on one point, move ahead by mean of a point to land on that point and have variance 0 → likelihood infinity

<br>
The original problem isnt solved directly, its approached from a surrogate surface that touches the true one
<br><br>


**GMM assumes data comes from overlapping distributions, and EM is the minimal machinery needed to fit such models when component identity is hidden**

---
## Math / Implementation:

**Parameters per component** :
1) mean $(\mu)$ (random data points)
2) variance $(\Sigma_k)$ (covariance)
3) mixing weight $(\pi_k)$ (intially all 1/K)
   
<br>

**Observed data log likelihood** →<br>      

 $L(\theta)=\sum_{i=1}^N \log(\sum_{k=1}^K \pi_k N(x_i|\mu_k,\Sigma_k))$ 

Its non convex , coupled across components, unbounded above

**For each point:**

- Compute how likely that point is under that component's Gaussian plus the component's mixing weight (in log-space) → normalize

Result would be an array of how much each component owns a point
<br> 

**For each component:**
- Add up its responsibilities, update mixing weight as a fraction of responsibility 
- mean as the responsibility-weighted average
- covariance as the responsibility-weighted scatter around the new mean (add some small scaled identity to keep it invertible)

Evaluate the observed log-likelihood, stop according to tolerance or max iterations
